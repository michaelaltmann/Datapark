Data Park is a tool that provides what-if statistics on the effects of new construction parking projections. It was developed as part of the 2013 Hack for MN. See my blog for details.

In its current version it is really a prototype that serves as a conversation starter about how communities could benefit from ways to visualize parking. Imagine that you and your neighbors are considering a new development project, called "Option 1" which will be a large condo on the south side of 46 St, between Aldrich and Bryant. It will require an average of 20 cars parked on the street.

Select when and where you are concerned about parking and how far you are willing to walk to a parking space.

Note: Currently, the application does not really use the parameters that you select or the location you click. It always shows results for parking within 100 meters of the intersection of 46 and Bryant.
